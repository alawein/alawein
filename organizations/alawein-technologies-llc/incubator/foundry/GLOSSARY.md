## A

### ARPU
**Average Revenue Per User**

Calculation: Total Revenue Ã· Number of Users

Example: If you have 100 users and make $500/month, ARPU = $5/user

Why it matters: Tells you how much money each user generates on average. Higher is better.

Related: MRR, LTV, CAC

---

### Assumptions
**Things you believe to be true but haven't validated**

Example: "Students will pay $99/month for this" (assumption) vs "5 students agreed to pre-pay $99" (validated)

Why it matters: Assumptions are dangerous. Validation turns assumptions into facts.

Related: Validation, Pivot

---

## B

### Beta Users / Beta Testing
**Early users who test product before public launch**

Who: Usually 10-50 early adopters or pre-sale customers

What they do: Use product, give feedback, identify bugs

Why it matters: Real users find problems you didn't anticipate. Makes launch smoother.

Related: MVP, Pre-sales, Product-Market Fit

---

### Burn Rate
**How fast you spend money**

Calculation: Monthly Expenses

Example: If you spend $5K/month and have $50K in the bank, you'll run out in 10 months

Why it matters: Tells you how long you can operate. Need revenue before money runs out.

Related: Runway, Profitability

---

## C

### CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
**How much it costs to acquire one customer**

Calculation: Total Marketing Spend Ã· New Customers Acquired

Example: Spend $1,000 on ads, get 10 customers = $100 CAC

Why it matters: If CAC > LTV, you'll never be profitable. Should be CAC < LTV/3

Related: LTV, Unit Economics, Payback Period

---

### Churn Rate / Churn
**Percentage of customers who cancel/leave each month**

Calculation: (Customers Lost Ã· Customers at Month Start) Ã— 100

Example: Started with 100 users, 5 cancelled = 5% churn

Good churn: <5% monthly | Bad churn: >10% monthly

Why it matters: Even great growth looks bad if churn is high. Retention = survival.

Related: Retention, Payback Period

---

### Cohort Analysis
**Grouping users by signup month to track their behavior over time**

Example: "Users from January have 40% retention after 3 months"

Why it matters: Shows if retention is improving. Identifies patterns in user behavior.

Related: Retention, Churn

---

### Conversion Rate
**Percentage of people who take desired action**

Examples:
- 10% of website visitors sign up = 10% conversion rate
- 20% of signups become paid = 20% conversion rate

Why it matters: 1% improvement in conversion = huge impact on revenue.

Related: Funnel, CAC

---

## D

### DAU (Daily Active Users)
**Number of unique users who engage with product daily**

Example: Day 1 = 50 DAU | Day 2 = 75 DAU | Day 3 = 60 DAU

Why it matters: Shows product is sticky. Users coming back.

Related: MAU, Engagement, Retention

---

### Default Mode
**What happens if you don't make any changes**

Example: If 50 businesses fail, default mode = you fail too

Why it matters: Reminds you that doing nothing = failure. Change is necessary.

Related: Pivot, Iteration

## G

### Go-to-Market Strategy
**How you'll reach and acquire customers**

Example: "We'll launch on Product Hunt, then build community, then paid ads"

Includes: Marketing, sales, partnerships, distribution strategy

Why it matters: Great product + bad go-to-market = failure. Both matter.

Related: Growth Strategy, Marketing Plan

## I

### Iteration
**Small changes and improvements based on feedback**

Example: User feedback = "It's too complicated" â†’ Simplify UI â†’ Collect feedback â†’ Repeat

Why it matters: Perfection is impossible. Quick iteration beats slow perfection.

## J

### Jobs-to-be-Done
**The underlying job your customer is trying to accomplish**

Example:
- Job: "Help me get better at public speaking"
- Solution could be: App, coach, course, community, etc.

Why it matters: Focus on the job, not the solution. Understand the real need.

Related: Problem Definition, Value Proposition

## K

### Key Metrics
**The 3-5 numbers that matter most for your business**

Example for SaaS:
1. Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
3. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
4. Churn Rate
5. Growth Rate

Why it matters: Obsess over the right metrics. Wrong metrics = wrong decisions.

Related: Metrics Dashboard, KPI

---

## L

### LTV (Lifetime Value)
**Total profit you'll make from one customer**

Calculation: (Average Monthly Revenue per User Ã— Average Customer Lifetime) - CAC

Example: Customer pays $99/month for 12 months before canceling = $1,188 LTV

Why it matters: If LTV < CAC, you'll never be profitable. LTV should be 3-5x CAC.

Related: CAC, Unit Economics, Payback Period

---

### Launch
**Going public / making product available to non-beta users**

Before launch: Private, limited users, iterating heavily

After launch: Public, anyone can use, customer support required

Why it matters: Huge milestone. Scary and exciting.

Related: MVP, Public Launch, Go-to-Market

---

## M

### MAU (Monthly Active Users)
**Unique users who engage at least once in a month**

Example: App has 1,000 MAU = 1,000 different people used it this month

Why it matters: Shows user base size. More reliable than DAU for evaluating growth.

Related: DAU, Engagement, Retention

### Monetization
**How you make money**

Examples:
- Freemium (free + paid features)
- Subscription ($9-99/month)

### MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
**Smallest version of product that solves the core problem**

Examples:
- Google started with "search one database"
- Dropbox started with a video, no product
- Instagram started with square photos + filters only

Why it matters: Get feedback fast. Don't build features you're not sure about.

Related: Iteration, Beta Testing, Product-Market Fit

---

### MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
**Predictable revenue you receive each month**

Calculation: (Number of Paid Users) Ã— (Average Monthly Price)

Example: 100 users at $99/month = $9,900 MRR

Why it matters: Most important metric for SaaS. Shows business health.

Related: ARPU, ARR, Revenue Growth

## O

### Onboarding
**Process of getting new users to "aha moment"**

Good onboarding: New user can use core feature in <5 minutes

Bad onboarding: New user confused, leaves in <2 minutes

Why it matters: First impression = critical. Bad onboarding kills growth.

Related: User Experience, Product-Market Fit

---

## P

### Pivot
**Change fundamental approach based on learnings**

Examples:
- Change target customer (researchers â†’ students)
- Change product features (interview tool â†’ community)
- Change business model (subscription â†’ freemium)

Why it matters: Validation fails â†’ you don't build wrong thing for 6 months

Related: Validation, Iteration, Assumption

---

### Pre-Sales
**Collecting payment before you ship product**

Example: During validation, 10 people agree to pay $99 after you ship

Why it matters: Proves people will actually pay. Not just interest, but commitment.

Related: Validation, MVP Launch

---

### Product-Market Fit
**When product resonates with market so strongly that growth becomes easy**

Signs:
- Users can't live without you
- Organic word-of-mouth growth
- 10%+ month-over-month growth
- Low churn (<5%)
- High NPS (>50)

Why it matters: Most important milestone. Before this = survival. After this = growth.

Related: Validation, Growth, Scale

---

### Profitability
**When revenue > expenses**

Break-even point: When revenue = expenses

Why it matters: Without profitability, you eventually run out of money.

Related: Unit Economics, Burn Rate, Runway

---

## R

### Retention
**Percentage of customers who stay**

Calculation: (Users Still Here Ã· Users at Start) Ã— 100

Example: Start with 100 users, 95 still here after 1 month = 95% retention

Good retention: >90% monthly | Bad retention: <85% monthly

Why it matters: Retention is easier than acquisition. Happy users = growth.

Related: Churn, Cohort Analysis, CAC

---

### Revenue Model
**How your business makes money**

Examples:
- SaaS subscription
- Freemium with premium features
- Marketplace commission
- Enterprise licensing
- Advertising

Why it matters: Can't scale without sustainable revenue model.

Related: Monetization, Pricing, Profitability

---

### Runway
**How many months you can operate with current cash**

Calculation: Cash in bank Ã· Monthly burn rate

Example: $50K in bank Ã· $5K monthly burn = 10 months runway

Why it matters: Tells you how much time to reach profitability.

Related: Burn Rate, Profitability, Fundraising

## S

### SaaS (Software as a Service)
**Software delivered as subscription over internet**

Examples: Slack, Salesforce, Notion, Google Workspace

Characteristics: Monthly/yearly billing, cloud-based, automatic updates

Why it matters: Most scalable software business model.

Related: Subscription, Monetization

---

### Segment / Market Segment
**Specific group of customers with similar needs**

Example:
- Segment 1: Individual researchers
- Segment 2: Academic institutions
- Segment 3: Corporations

Why it matters: Different segments have different needs. Don't try to serve everyone.

Related: Target Market, Positioning

---

## T

### Target Customer / Target Market
**Specific people you're building for**

Example: "Small research teams at top 100 US universities"

Specificity matters. Vague = failure. Specific = focus.

Why it matters: Clear target = better product, better marketing, better sales.

Related: Segment, Personas, Value Proposition

---

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
**Total revenue available if you captured 100% of market**

Example: If there are 100,000 researchers and each pays $100/year = $10M TAM

Why it matters: Shows business potential. Bigger TAM = more venture capital interest.

Related: Market Size, SAM, SOM

---

## U

### Unit Economics
**Revenue and costs at the customer level**

Example:
- Price: $99/month
- COGS: $10/month (cost to serve)
- Gross Margin: $89/month
- CAC: $200 (marketing cost)
- Payback Period: 2.25 months

Why it matters: If unit economics don't work, scaling makes it worse.

Related: CAC, LTV, Profitability, Pricing

---

## V

### Validation
**Proving assumptions are correct before building**

Validation process:
1. Talk to 20 people
2. Ask deep questions about problem
3. Show solution mockup
4. Ask if they'd pay
5. Collect money before building

Why it matters: Validation before building = 50% success rate. No validation = 5%.

Related: Pre-sales, MVP, Pivot

---

### Validation-First
**Foundry core methodology: validate before building**

Steps:
1. Week 1-2: Identify problem (talk to 20 users)
2. Week 2-3: Show solution (mockups)
3. Week 3-4: Get pre-sales (collect payment)
4. Week 4: Decide GO/PIVOT/STOP

Why it matters: Prevents building something nobody wants. Saves 6 months.

Related: Validation, Lean Startup, MVP

---

### Value Proposition
**Reason customer should buy your product instead of alternatives**

Example: "Get research feedback in minutes, not weeks"

Must be clear, specific, and believable.

Why it matters: Without clear value prop, customers choose competitor.

Related: Differentiation, Positioning, Product-Market Fit
