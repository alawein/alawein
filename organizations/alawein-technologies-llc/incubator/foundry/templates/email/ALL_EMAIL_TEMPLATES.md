## 1. Initial Interview Request (Cold)

**Use when:** Reaching out to strangers (Google Scholar, LinkedIn, conference lists)

**Subject Lines (A/B test these):**
- Quick question about [THEIR RESEARCH AREA]
- 15 minutes to learn about [THEIR WORK]?
- Research on [TOPIC] - your input would be valuable
- [MUTUAL CONNECTION] suggested I reach out

**Email:**
```
Subject: Quick question about [THEIR SPECIFIC WORK/RESEARCH AREA]

Hi [First Name],

I came across your [recent paper on X / work at Company / LinkedIn profile] and was impressed by [SPECIFIC DETAIL - show you actually looked].

I'm researching how [TARGET USER TYPE] currently [DO SPECIFIC TASK/HANDLE SPECIFIC PROBLEM], and your experience would be really valuable.

Would you have 15 minutes for a quick call this week? I'm trying to understand:
- Current workflow for [TASK]
- Pain points and frustrations
- Tools you're currently using

Not selling anything - genuinely trying to learn from people who [DO THE WORK].

If you're open to it, here's my calendar: [CALENDLY LINK]
Or reply with times that work for you!

Thanks for considering,
[Your Name]
[Your Title/Affiliation if relevant]
[LinkedIn profile if relevant]

P.S. - Happy to send you a summary of insights from all the interviews!
```

**Variations:**

**If targeting academics:**
```
I came across your recent paper "[PAPER TITLE]" and found your approach to [SPECIFIC METHODOLOGY] really interesting.
```

**If offering incentive:**
```
As a thank you for your time, I'd like to send you a $10 Amazon gift card.
```

**If you have mutual connection:**
```
[MUTUAL CONNECTION NAME] suggested I reach out - they mentioned you'd have great insights on [TOPIC].
```

## üéØ HOW TO USE THESE TEMPLATES

### 1. Don't Copy-Paste Blindly
- Customize [BRACKETS] with your specifics
- Add personal details (references to their work)
- Match their tone (formal for academics, casual for startup folks)

### 2. Test Subject Lines
- A/B test 2-3 different subject lines
- Track open rates (aim for 30%+)
- Double down on what works

### 3. Keep Emails Short
- Max 150 words for cold emails
- Max 200 words for updates
- People skim, so make it scannable

### 4. Always Have Clear CTA
- One ask per email
- Make it dead simple (click this link, reply with answer)
- Tell them exactly what to do

### 5. Follow Up
- Send reminder after 3-5 days if no response
- Don't follow up more than twice
- Move on if they don't respond
