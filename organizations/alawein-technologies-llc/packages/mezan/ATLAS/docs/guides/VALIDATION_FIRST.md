## üéØ What "Validation" Means

Validation = **Evidence that people will pay for your solution**

NOT validation:
- ‚ùå "My friends think it's a good idea"
- ‚ùå "50 people signed up for the waitlist"
- ‚ùå "Reddit upvoted my post"
- ‚ùå "I got positive feedback"

REAL validation:
- ‚úÖ 10+ people pre-paid before you built anything
- ‚úÖ 30 user interviews showing consistent pain (8+/10 severity)
- ‚úÖ People already paying $X/month for partial solutions
- ‚úÖ You can articulate the exact job your product is being hired to do

**If you can't get 10 people to pre-pay $99, you don't have a business.**

## üìã The 4-Week Validation Sprint

### Week 1: Problem Discovery (20 hours)
**Goal:** Understand if the problem is real and painful

#### Day 1-2: Research & Preparation (4 hours)
- [ ] Read the Customer Development guide (`.meta/guides/CUSTOMER_DEVELOPMENT.md`)
- [ ] Copy the Problem Discovery interview script
- [ ] Identify where to find target users (Reddit, Twitter, academic forums)
- [ ] Create list of 50 potential interviewees

**Deliverable:** Interview script + list of 50 prospects

---

#### Day 3-4: Recruit Participants (4 hours)
- [ ] Email 20 people in your network (warm intros)
- [ ] Post on 5 relevant subreddits asking for interviews
- [ ] Tweet asking for interviews
- [ ] Cold email 20 researchers from Google Scholar

**Template (Reddit):**
```
Title: PhD/Researcher? Help me understand your workflow (15 min chat)

I'm researching how researchers validate their hypotheses before publication.

If you've ever worried about getting destroyed by reviewers, I'd love to chat
for 15 minutes to understand your process.

Not selling anything - genuinely trying to understand the problem.

DM me if you're open to a quick call. Happy to send a $10 Amazon gift card
as thanks.
```

**Goal:** Schedule 10 interviews by end of Week 1

**Red flag:** If you can't find 10 people willing to talk ‚Üí Your target market may not exist or isn't accessible

---

#### Day 5-7: Conduct 10 Interviews (12 hours)
- [ ] Conduct 10 problem discovery interviews (use script from Customer Development guide)
- [ ] Take detailed notes for each (use template)
- [ ] Record if they consent (Zoom/Google Meet)
- [ ] Send thank-you emails + ask for referrals

**What to listen for:**
- **Pain severity:** Is this an 8+/10 problem?
- **Current solutions:** Are they already paying for tools?
- **Urgency:** Do they need a solution NOW or "someday maybe"?
- **Specific examples:** Can they describe the last time this problem hurt them?

**Success criteria for Week 1:**
- ‚úÖ Completed 10 interviews
- ‚úÖ Identified consistent pain point (mentioned by 7+/10 people)
- ‚úÖ Average pain severity: 7+/10
- ‚úÖ People already spending money in this category

**Failure criteria:**
- ‚ùå Can't recruit 10 people ‚Üí Market may not exist or isn't reachable
- ‚ùå Pain severity <6/10 ‚Üí Not urgent enough to pay for
- ‚ùå No one currently pays for solutions ‚Üí No budget allocated
- ‚ùå Everyone describes problem differently ‚Üí Poorly defined

**Decision point:** If you hit failure criteria, STOP or PIVOT now. Don't proceed to Week 2.

---

### Week 2: Deep Dive & Solution Hypothesis (20 hours)

#### Day 8-9: Analyze Week 1 Data (4 hours)
- [ ] Review all 10 interview notes
- [ ] Identify top 3 pain points (by frequency)
- [ ] List current solutions people use
- [ ] Calculate average willingness to pay
- [ ] Create Jobs-to-be-Done canvas

**Template:**
```
WEEK 1 ANALYSIS

Interviews: 10
Average pain severity: __/10

TOP 3 PAIN POINTS:
1. ___________ (mentioned by __/10 people, avg severity: __/10)
2. ___________ (mentioned by __/10 people, avg severity: __/10)
3. ___________ (mentioned by __/10 people, avg severity: __/10)

CURRENT SOLUTIONS:
- ___________ ($__/mo, used by __ people)
- ___________ ($__/mo, used by __ people)

STATED WILLINGNESS TO PAY:
- Range: $__ - $__/month
- Average: $__/month

JOBS TO BE DONE:
Functional: "I need to ___________"
Emotional: "I want to feel ___________"
Social: "I want others to think ___________"

DECISION: GO / PIVOT / STOP
Reasoning: ___________
```

**GO criteria:**
- 7+/10 people mentioned same pain
- Average pain severity 7+/10
- People already paying for partial solutions
- Clear job-to-be-done identified

**PIVOT criteria:**
- Some pain but inconsistent
- Different market segment might be better fit
- Solution hypothesis needs adjustment

**STOP criteria:**
- Pain <6/10 average
- No existing spending in category
- Can't recruit enough people

---

#### Day 10-11: Design Solution Mockups (6 hours)
- [ ] Sketch 3-5 key screens (use Figma, Excalidraw, or pen+paper)
- [ ] Write 3 core features (must-haves only)
- [ ] Create 30-second pitch describing the solution
- [ ] Build simple landing page (Carrd, Webflow - 1 hour to build)

**Landing page must have:**
1. Headline: "Solve [PROBLEM] in [TIME/EFFORT]"
2. 3 key benefits (aligned with top 3 pains from Week 1)
3. Mockup/screenshot
4. Pricing: "Early access: $99 one-time (normally $199)"
5. Email capture form
6. FAQ section

**DO NOT BUILD THE PRODUCT YET.**

#### Day 12-14: Conduct 10 Solution Validation Interviews (10 hours)
- [ ] Recruit 10 MORE people (new people, not same as Week 1)
- [ ] Show them mockups
- [ ] Run Solution Validation interview script
- [ ] Test pricing ($10, $25, $50, $99 - see what resonates)
- [ ] Ask: "Would you pre-pay $99 for 3 months to get early access?"

**What to listen for:**
- **Excitement level:** "When can I use this?" vs "That's interesting"
- **Missing features:** What would make this a must-have?
- **Willingness to pay:** Do they hesitate at price?
- **Pre-sale readiness:** Are they ready to pay NOW?

**Success criteria for Week 2:**
- ‚úÖ 7+/10 people show genuine excitement ("When can I use this?")
- ‚úÖ 5+/10 people say they would pre-pay $99
- ‚úÖ Clear differentiation from existing tools identified
- ‚úÖ Must-have features confirmed

**Failure criteria:**
- ‚ùå Polite interest only ("That's nice")
- ‚ùå Everyone wants different features (no consensus)
- ‚ùå Price resistance ("Too expensive for what it is")
- ‚ùå "I'd wait for reviews" (no urgency)

**Decision point:** If failure, PIVOT solution or STOP. Don't proceed to Week 3.

## üö® Common Validation Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Asking the Wrong Questions
‚ùå "Would you use this?" (Everyone says yes to be nice)
‚úÖ "What did you do last time you had this problem?"

‚ùå "Is this a good idea?" (Opinions are worthless)
‚úÖ "Have you paid for solutions to this problem?"

### Mistake #2: Confusing Interest with Demand
‚ùå "50 people signed up for waitlist" ‚Üí Means nothing
‚úÖ "10 people pre-paid $99" ‚Üí Real demand signal

‚ùå "Everyone I talked to loved it" ‚Üí Selection bias
‚úÖ "7/10 strangers from Reddit pre-paid" ‚Üí Validated

### Mistake #3: Skipping Straight to Building
‚ùå "I'll just build it and see if people use it" ‚Üí 95% failure rate
‚úÖ "I'll validate demand, THEN build" ‚Üí 50% success rate

### Mistake #4: Listening to Feedback, Not Watching Behavior
‚ùå "They said they'd pay $50/month" ‚Üí People lie (unintentionally)
‚úÖ "They pre-paid $99" ‚Üí Behavior never lies

### Mistake #5: Validating with the Wrong People
‚ùå Asking friends/family (they'll be nice)
‚ùå Asking on HackerNews (not your target market)
‚úÖ Asking real researchers who have the exact problem

### Mistake #6: Stopping Too Early
‚ùå "I did 3 interviews, seems good" ‚Üí Not enough data
‚úÖ "I did 20 interviews + pre-sale experiment" ‚Üí Real evidence

### Mistake #7: Not Stopping When You Should
‚ùå "Only 1 pre-sale but I'll build anyway" ‚Üí Wasting 6 months
‚úÖ "Only 1 pre-sale, I'm pivoting" ‚Üí Saved 6 months

## ‚úÖ Validation Checklist

**Use this checklist to ensure you've really validated:**

### Problem Validation
- [ ] Conducted 20+ user interviews
- [ ] Identified consistent pain (70%+ of interviewees)
- [ ] Average pain severity 7+/10
- [ ] People already spending money in this category
- [ ] Can articulate specific job-to-be-done
- [ ] Total addressable market >10,000 potential users

### Solution Validation
- [ ] Showed mockups to 10+ potential users
- [ ] 70%+ showed genuine excitement
- [ ] Must-have features identified (consensus)
- [ ] Clear differentiation from existing tools
- [ ] Pricing validated (no sticker shock)

### Demand Validation (THE CRITICAL TEST)
- [ ] Built landing page with pre-sale offer
- [ ] Sent offer to all interviewees
- [ ] Promoted in target communities
- [ ] Achieved 10+ pre-sales OR have clear reason why not
- [ ] Conversion rate >2% (if traffic was sufficient)
- [ ] Low refund/cancellation rate

### Market Validation
- [ ] Calculated realistic TAM (total addressable market)
- [ ] Identified customer acquisition channels
- [ ] Estimated CAC (customer acquisition cost)
- [ ] Projected LTV (lifetime value)
- [ ] LTV:CAC ratio >3:1
- [ ] Unit economics make sense

### Execution Validation
- [ ] Can build MVP in 8-12 weeks
- [ ] Have required technical skills OR co-founder
- [ ] Can support product for 12+ months
- [ ] Have go-to-market plan
- [ ] Committed to talking to users continuously

**If you can't check 80%+ of these boxes, you haven't validated.**
